Smoking Prevalence from 1980 to 2000

The period from 1980 to 2000 witnessed a significant shift in the fight against smoking prevalence. Initiatives such as the 'Great American Smokeout' in 1980, 'World No Tobacco Day' established by the World Health Organization in 1987, and the 'Truth Campaign' in the 2000s reshaped public perception of smoking.

These efforts exposed deceptive marketing strategies and empowered youth to combat the tobacco industry, ultimately leading to reduced smoking rates and marking a crucial era in the battle against tobacco use.

Male versus Female Smoking Trends: A Population Perspective

This Bubble Plot sheds light on the significance of examining male and female smokers globally. With approximately 1.1 billion smokers worldwide, a substantial gender disparity exists, with higher smoking rates among males.

Continental Smoking Trends: Identifying the Pinnacle and Pitfall Nations

This bump chart analyzes the smoking habits of countries across continents over time. It sheds light on global disparities in tobacco use, emphasizing best-performing nations with low rates and worst-performing ones with high rates, spanning a significant time period.

Market Cap Polar Chart: Unveiling the Tobacco Titans

The global tobacco industry encompasses over 7 million metric tons of annual production and a $800 billion market. Despite its economic impact, tobacco use causes nearly 8 million deaths annually, and approximately 20% of adults worldwide are smokers.
